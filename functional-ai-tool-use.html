Overview

Why platform literacy matters

AI tools are multiplying fast — and choosing the wrong one for a task wastes time, produces weaker results, and erodes trust. Knowing what each platform is optimised for, and when to switch between them, is one of the most immediately practical AI skills you can build.

Major Platforms

Know your tools

Each major AI platform has distinct strengths. The best AI-ready professionals aren't loyal to one tool — they're fluent across several.

🤖

ChatGPT

OpenAI

💠

Microsoft Copilot

Microsoft

Embedded in Office 365. Best for summarising meetings, drafting emails in Outlook, and analysing data in Excel spreadsheets.

✦

Gemini

Google

Integrated with Google Workspace. Best for real-time web-grounded research, Gmail drafts, and Docs/Slides collaboration.

⚡

Claude

Anthropic

🔍

Perplexity

Perplexity AI

🛠

GitHub Copilot

GitHub / OpenAI

IDE-embedded code assistant. Best for autocomplete, generating boilerplate, explaining existing code, and writing tests.

Practical Tips

Getting more from every platform

Regardless of which tool you use, these habits will consistently improve your results and help you build genuine platform fluency.

1

Start with the context, not the question

Before asking the AI anything, give it relevant background: your role, the audience, the format you need, and any constraints. "I'm a marketing manager writing a one-page summary for our CFO about our Q3 campaign results" produces far better output than "summarise this."

2

Iterate, don't regenerate

When output isn't quite right, refine it in the same conversation rather than starting over. Say "make this more concise" or "change the tone to be more formal" — the model retains context and improves on its previous answer more effectively than a fresh prompt.

3

Use the tool where your work already lives

The highest-value AI integrations are the ones embedded in your existing workflow. Copilot in Word, Gemini in Docs, or Copilot in your IDE. Switching context to a separate browser tab adds friction and reduces the value of AI assistance.

4

Verify before you send

AI platforms can confidently produce incorrect facts, outdated statistics, or fabricated citations. Before forwarding AI-generated content to a colleague or client, spot-check any specific claims, numbers, or attributed quotes against a primary source.
