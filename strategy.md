# Functional AI Proficiency
## Build consistent practice, showcase applied work, and reuse strong prompts.

### Steps

1. Start a daily AI tool practice habit: Practice one AI tool every day for a real work task, such as drafting, summarizing, researching, or organizing information.
2. Create a Project Portfolio: Compile a list of completed projects that demonstrate how you have applied standard AI tools to specific professional workflows.
3. Build a Prompt Library: Develop and document a set of "reusable prompt templates" that use specific personas and constraints to improve the "effectiveness" of your AI instructions.

### Resources

- [Google Prompting Essentials](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/prompting-essentials-google)
- [Learn Prompting](https://learnprompting.org/)
- [IBM Generative AI: Prompt Engineering Basics](https://www.coursera.org/learn/generative-ai-prompt-engineering-for-everyone)

# Strategic Intelligence
## Make AI part of how you scope work, map value, and read your industry.

### Steps

1. Build a habit: before starting any significant task, ask 'Could AI help with any part of this?'
2. Map AI value: Pick one workflow in your job. Break it into steps and label each as: automate, augment, or human-only. Focus on where AI changes decisions, not just speed.
3. Start mapping AI to your industry: read 2–3 articles per week about AI's impact on your specific sector. Search '[your industry] + AI use cases'

### Resources

- [Elements of AI](https://www.elementsofai.com/)
- [Wharton AI Strategy and Governance](https://www.coursera.org/learn/wharton-ai-strategy-governance)
- [MIT Sloan Management Review – AI & Machine Learning](https://sloanreview.mit.edu/topic/ai-machine-learning/)

# Ethical Stewardship
## Review outputs rigorously, stress-test prompts, and deepen ethics training.

### Steps

1. Create a personal checklist: Use a checklist for reviewing AI outputs: accuracy, bias, privacy, and source quality.
2. Complete a formal AI ethics or responsible AI training: Courses like MIT's "Ethics of AI" or Google's Responsible AI practices give you frameworks for bias detection, fairness assessment, and data privacy
3. Rephrase prompts: Test the same prompt with different wording or perspectives to see whether the model gives biased or uneven results.

### Resources

- [fast.ai Practical Data Ethics](https://ethics.fast.ai/)
- [Kaggle Intro to AI Ethics](https://www.kaggle.com/learn/intro-to-ai-ethics)
- [DataCamp AI Ethics](https://www.datacamp.com/courses/ai-ethics)

# Critical Human Skills
## Strengthen explanation, adaptability, and human-led facilitation with AI.

### Steps

1. Practice Communication: explain an AI-generated idea to another person or AI companion in simple terms, then refine it based on their feedback.
2. Divergent thinking: Work on one unfamiliar tool, topic, or workflow each week to build adaptability and comfort with change.
3. Lead Human-AI Brainstorming: Facilitate a team session where AI generates raw ideas, but you use "creative thinking" and "emotional intelligence" to refine them for a specific "human purpose"

### Resources

- [Learning How to Learn](https://www.coursera.org/learn/learning-how-to-learn)
- [Creative Thinking: Techniques and Tools for Success](https://www.coursera.org/learn/creative-thinking-techniques-and-tools-for-success)
- [IBM Solving Problems with Creative and Critical Thinking](https://www.coursera.org/learn/solving-problems-with-creative-and-critical-thinking)
